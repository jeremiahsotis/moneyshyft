# Workflow Context

## A. Commitment workflow

1. staff identifies a financial assistance need
2. staff creates a commitment record
3. commitment may be tied to a case, person, household, or future service context
4. commitment remains tracked until fulfilled, canceled, or expired

## B. Disbursement workflow

1. approved commitment moves toward payment or assistance delivery
2. staff records disbursement
3. disbursement is linked to the originating commitment
4. status and amount history are preserved

## C. Collaborative funding workflow

1. one assistance need requires support from multiple funders
2. separate funding participation records are added
3. total assistance remains traceable across all contributing sources
4. workflow shows whether the need is fully funded, partially funded, or still pending

## D. Voucher issuance workflow

1. staff creates a voucher from case/service/program context
2. voucher amount, type, restrictions, and expiration are stored
3. voucher becomes available for later redemption
4. voucher audit trail is preserved

## E. Voucher redemption workflow

1. voucher is presented for redemption
2. system checks status and balance rules
3. voucher is marked redeemed, partially redeemed, expired, canceled, or exhausted as applicable
4. redemption history is stored

## F. Future thrift redemption workflow

1. thrift workflow receives voucher
2. FinanceCore validates voucher status
3. redemption result is returned
4. thrift-side workflow records its own operational details separately

## G. Finance review workflow

1. supervisor or finance reviewer reviews commitments/disbursements/vouchers
2. statuses may be updated according to permissions
3. audit history remains visible
