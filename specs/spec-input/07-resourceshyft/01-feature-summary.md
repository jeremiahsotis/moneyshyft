# ResourceShyft — Feature Summary

## What is being built

ResourceShyft is the service discovery and community resource layer of ShyftUnity.

This feature introduces and formalizes:

- provider / organization directory
- service offering model
- service locations
- service categories and search metadata
- service availability representation
- service requirements visibility
- search UX and result ranking behavior
- launch actions from service detail into downstream workflows

ResourceShyft is not just a list of organizations. It is a structured service discovery system that helps staff identify the right service offering for a person or household and then move directly into action.

## Why now

After Documents + Evidence and the Eligibility Engine, the next operational need is practical service matching.

Without ResourceShyft:
- staff still rely on scattered lists and memory
- eligibility results are harder to turn into action
- service details remain inconsistent
- users cannot move smoothly from discovery to case/referral/program workflows

ResourceShyft turns service knowledge into usable operational infrastructure.

## Who uses this

Primary users:
- intake staff
- case managers
- Vincentians
- program managers
- supervisors

Secondary users:
- future partner organizations
- future public-facing service discovery users
- referral workflows
- future self-service experiences

## Success definition

A staff user should be able to:

1. search for services quickly
2. filter and refine results
3. understand what a service actually offers
4. see important availability and requirement information
5. understand whether a service is a likely fit
6. launch directly into downstream actions such as case follow-up, referral, program inquiry, or voucher-related flow later
